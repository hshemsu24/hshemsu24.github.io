ABOUT ME

Hello, my name is Hamza Shemsu, and I am currently a student at Amherst College, majoring in Computer Science. I come from a proud Ethiopian background and have grown up in the vibrant city of Cambridge, MA.

I have pursued coursework directly relevant to software engineering and development throughout my academic journey. I have completed courses in Algorithms, Data Structures, Machine Learning, and Computer Systems. These classes have equipped me with a strong foundation in problem-solving, efficient algorithms, and understanding the inner workings of computers. In the upcoming fall semester, I am excited to dive into the realms of Databases and Networks, further expanding my knowledge and skill set in the field.

While my coding expertise primarily lies in Java, HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, I am always eager to expand my knowledge and learn new languages and frameworks. Through my exploration of Machine Learning, I have gained familiarity with Python, and my study of Computer Systems has introduced me to C and Assembly. This versatility allows me to adapt to different programming languages and work comfortably across various software development domains.

Besides coding and programming, my passions extend to soccer, video games, photography, and the exploration of religion, particularly Islam. Looking ahead, my ultimate goal is to become a software engineer, leveraging my skills to make meaningful contributions in the realms of soccer, video games, and Islam. Whether it's creating innovative sports-related applications, developing immersive gaming experiences, or utilizing technology to facilitate educational resources on Islam, I aspire to combine my technical acumen with my passions to positively impact the world.
